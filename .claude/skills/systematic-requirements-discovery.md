# Systematic Requirements Discovery

## When to Use
- Ambiguous project requests
- User doesn't know what they need
- Assumptions need validation
- Completeness verification needed

## Process

### 1. Start with "Why"
Understand purpose before solution. Ask questions like:
- What problem are you trying to solve?
- What will success look like?
- Why is this important now?

### 2. Identify Stakeholders
Who is affected? Consider:
- Direct users/customers
- Business stakeholders
- Technical team members
- Support/operations teams

### 3. Socratic Questioning
Guide discovery through questions rather than assumptions:
- Ask open-ended questions
- Follow up on answers with clarifying questions
- Challenge vague statements gently
- Explore alternatives and trade-offs

### 4. Validate Assumptions
Challenge what's taken for granted:
- List implicit assumptions
- Question each one: "Is this actually true?"
- Explore impact if assumption is wrong
- Get stakeholder confirmation

### 5. Check Completeness
Ensure all angles covered:
- Scope boundaries: what's in/out?
- Edge cases: what about unusual scenarios?
- Constraints: time, budget, technical, regulatory?
- Success metrics: how will we measure success?

### 6. Confirm Understanding
Verify with stakeholder:
- Summarize what you learned
- Ask if anything was missed
- Confirm priorities and trade-offs
- Document agreements
